Business FAQ

What are healthy profit margins in restoration?
In the restoration industry, a healthy Net Profit Margin typically ranges between 15% to 25%. Mitigation services often command higher margins (up to 40%+) due to specialized equipment, while reconstruction typically has lower margins (10-15%) due to subcontracting costs.
What's included in COGS?
Cost of Goods Sold includes direct costs tied to revenue: field labor (technician wages), materials (antimicrobials, drywall, flooring), and equipment rentals or direct fuel costs for service vehicles.
COGS vs Operating Expenses?
COGS are direct costs that vary with work volume. Operating Expenses are indirect costs/overhead needed regardless of volume: office rent, insurance, marketing, and administrative salaries.
How is Net Margin calculated?
Net Margin = (Net Profit / Total Revenue) × 100. It represents how many cents of every dollar earned becomes profit after all expenses.
